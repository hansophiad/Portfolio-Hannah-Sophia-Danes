GRATEFRUIT

A Gratitude App

Gratefruit came about as I saw a gap in the market - whilst I found existing apps promoting gratitude (or similar), I believed there was a lot of room for improvement.

So I set out to design an uplifting app that acts as a gratitude journal, meeting the needs of its users by providing the necessary tools & inspiration via a tasteful & calming product.

A Unique Concept

Gratefruit includes an onboarding explanation detailing the concept of the app & how it works. Whilst it was important to me that the app was simple (so as to keep the user focused on the core function), I wanted to tastefully make use of gamification as a way of keeping users engaged.

The idea is that users add regular entries detailing their moments of gratitude. This could be a paragraph or a single word, a photo, a recording or perhaps all of the above! Each entry is called a "scafie" (what Zimbabwean's call an individual segment of citrus) and users can 'favourite' these, by adding scafies to their "Gratefruit Marmalade". The glossary & explanation are easily accessible, to ensure the user understands the concept.

The Look & Feel

My aesthetic vision for Gratefruit was inspired by how I wanted future users to feel when using the app - calm, inspired & optimistic. I intended for Gratefruit’s brand identity & app interface to be a reflection of the positive effect of active gratitude.

Certain style aspects of the brand are universal, but for key UI elements I followed the Apple & Material Design guidelines for the iOS & Android versions, respectively. By applying the Gratefruit visual identity to familiar patterns, I’m ensuring the best experience for users.

Key Features

In order to make Gratefruit as user-friendly as possible, I included the function enabling users to add photos and/or recordings to their gratitude entries (“scafies”). No time to type? No problem.

Users can 'favourite' cherished gratitde entries by adding "scafies" to their "Gratefruit Marmalade" - which can be browsed and/or searched.

The Finished Product

I thoroughly enjoyed creating Gratefruit, though it wasn’t a straight line from start to finish.

Initially, I found it difficult to communicate the concept in a succinct & universally understandable manner, but by testing it with peers & potential users, I was able to work it out.

In building out the app and testing, I had to make some calls that meant deviating from my original idea - no login for increased privacy, no timeline for simplicity.

I’m confident that Gratefruit meets the project objective by offering an uplifting app for the active practice of gratitude. From the unique concept and helpful functionalities, to the calming yet inspiring design, Gratefruit is an app users will certainly like.
